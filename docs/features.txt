EZVAC ALPHA 1.0 FEATURES
========================

EZvac is a standalone Paper 1.21.1 HVAC and indoor-climate plugin. Climate
systems are identified only by world and group label.


SYSTEM MODEL
------------

- One sign thermostat is the controller for one world-scoped HVAC group.
- A group may have one optional settings-panel sign. Without one, its operating
  profile is always NORMAL and matches the stock variable-speed behavior.
- A group may contain any number of heat pumps, air conditioners, or furnaces.
- Heat pumps cool and heat; air conditioners cool only; furnaces heat only.
- Dispensers are authoritative actuators. Registered equipment cancels vanilla
  dispense behavior so inventory contents and redstone cannot fight the plugin.
- Equipment does not consume buckets, fuel, water, power, or other resources.
- Heat pumps and ACs use water as their visible output. Furnaces use lava.
- Only the exact output source created and recorded by EZvac is retracted.
  Player fluid is never adopted merely because it is in front of a dispenser.
- Shared output blocks have one deterministic winner. Losing units stop and
  release their output before the winner starts.
- Rotated dispensers clean their previous owned output before claiming the new
  facing block. Unloaded cleanup is persisted and retried later.


CONTROLLER ORDER AND SAFETY
---------------------------

Every controller pass has explicit phases:

1. Validate equipment that physically delivered during the elapsed interval.
2. Advance compressor/blower RPM and calculate real delivered capacity.
3. Integrate each thermostat's room temperature.
4. Apply hysteresis, minimum-run, and minimum-off decisions.
5. Stop invalid, incompatible, disabled, blocked, or losing actuators.
6. Clean rotated/shared outputs.
7. Start the deterministic actuator winners for the new demand.

A failure in one thermostat or equipment unit is logged and isolated. It does
not abort every HVAC system for that controller pass.

Default thermostat behavior:

- Cooling starts above target + 1.0 F.
- Heating starts below target - 1.0 F.
- Cooling stops at target - 0.3 F.
- Heating stops at target + 0.3 F.
- Minimum run time is 300 ticks (normally 15 seconds).
- Minimum off time is 200 ticks (normally 10 seconds).
- Player targets are clamped to 60-85 F.
- Control stop boundaries are not clamped to the player target range. A 60 F
  cooling setpoint can correctly stop at 59.7 F, and 85 F heating at 85.3 F.

If a thermostat sign is unloaded, removed, disabled, or has no compatible
loaded equipment, demand fails safe to idle. The cached biome profile and world
clock still allow its simulated room to drift without force-loading chunks.


THERMAL AND MOTOR PERFORMANCE
-----------------------------

- One full-capacity unit cools 3.75 F per Minecraft hour.
- One full-capacity unit heats 5.0 F per Minecraft hour.
- Idle rooms drift 1.25 F per Minecraft hour toward outdoor temperature.
- Active outdoor load is 0.50 F per Minecraft hour.
- One Minecraft climate hour is 1,000 server ticks.
- Multiple units use square-root diminishing returns, capped at 2x capacity.
- Thermal integration is partition-independent across outdoor and control
  boundaries. Splitting the same ticks into multiple calls gives the same result.
- Heat-pump and AC capacity varies from 40% to 100%. Outside the default 3 F
  approach band they request full output; inside it they progressively slow.
- Every running heat pump and AC in a group receives the same variable-speed
  command. Their RPM-derived capacities are summed, so combined cooling slows
  in sync as the group approaches its target.
- ECO caps variable compressors at 2,400 RPM, NORMAL at 3,600 RPM, and TURBO at
  4,200 RPM by default. All caps are configurable. NORMAL is byte-for-byte the
  same RPM curve used before operating profiles were introduced.
- Variable compressors ramp from a 1,200 RPM minimum. The thermal simulator
  uses the capacity derived from actual ramped RPM, not merely the requested
  capacity or a cosmetic RPM display value.
- Furnace blowers ramp to 1,100 RPM; furnace heat stays at full capacity.
- Fixed furnace capacity and variable heat-pump capacity are accounted for
  independently. A slowed heat pump never averages a furnace below 100%; both
  contributions are added before the overall multi-unit diminishing-return cap.
- Profiles never affect furnaces. Heat pumps and ACs share one group-level
  profile command, including mixed AC/heat-pump cooling systems.
- Very low startup RPM cannot create phantom heating/cooling or oscillate at an
  exact outdoor-temperature equilibrium.

Controller, thermal, variable-speed, airflow, and audio tuning values are
validated settings in config.yml. Invalid settings stop the plugin safely
instead of starting with an internally inconsistent controller.


OUTDOOR CLIMATE
---------------

Outdoor temperature follows Minecraft time and broad biome profiles:

- Snowy: 25 F noon / 5 F midnight
- Cold: 45 F noon / 28 F midnight
- Temperate: 72 F noon / 52 F midnight
- Warm: 85 F noon / 68 F midnight
- Hot: 100 F noon / 78 F midnight
- Nether: 112 F constant
- End: 30 F constant

Profiles are cached per thermostat, so an unloaded thermostat never causes a
chunk load merely to sample its biome.


VENTS AND AIRFLOW
-----------------

The persistent duct-link stick selects registered equipment and links ordinary
iron trapdoors to its group. Only explicitly linked trapdoors become vents.

- Each vent seeds six-directional airflow.
- Passable blocks carry air.
- Liquids and solid blocks stop air.
- Open doors/trapdoors pass air; closed ones stop it.
- Core distance 0-15 receives full thermostat temperature.
- Distance 16-30 blends progressively back to outdoor temperature.
- Beyond distance 30 there is no influence.

Airflow is event-invalidated and rebuilt incrementally. It never force-loads a
chunk, inspects at most 4,000 nodes per server tick by default, and caps a group
at 200,000 cells. Chunk events invalidate only vent systems whose possible
airflow radius intersects that chunk, instead of every system in the world.


DISPLAYS AND AUDIO
------------------

- Thermostat signs use a compact real-thermostat layout showing EZvac/ID,
  current room temperature, COOL/HEAT/IDLE/OFF, setpoint, live capacity, group,
  and an approximate ETA while actively heating or cooling.
- ETA is forward-simulated from the current equipment mix, actual RPM, future
  compressor modulation, outdoor load, and the same thermal rules as control.
- The target can be changed by command or by editing line 3 of the sign.
- Thermometers are independent point sensors with no stored group. Each reads
  its own sign block exactly like /hvac temperature: influenced vent temperature
  where airflow reaches it, blended edge temperature, or outdoor temperature.
  They may be placed outdoors or where no HVAC group exists.
- Typed RPM signs show active-unit average RPM, capacity, and running counts.
- Settings-panel signs show their system and ECO/NORMAL/TURBO profile. A normal
  right-click advances the profile; sneak-right-click moves backward. Removing
  the panel immediately restores the group to NORMAL.
- Display writes are fingerprinted so unchanged signs are not rewritten.

Startup and shutdown sounds remain equipment-specific. Running equipment emits
finite, spatial beacon ambience from each dispenser on a configurable cadence.
EZvac never performs broad sound/category stops, so it cannot silence an
unrelated beacon, furnace, or another plugin's audio.


PERSISTENCE
-----------

All thermostats, equipment, pending cleanup, vents, thermometers, RPM monitors,
and settings panels live in one versioned plugins/EZvac/hvac.yml file.

- Records use immutable world UUID + integer block coordinates.
- World unloads require no detach/rebind rewrite.
- Saves use a temporary file, backup, and atomic replacement when supported.
- If the main file is unreadable, the backup is restored.
- Invalid records are logged and preserved instead of overwritten.
- Loaded missing blocks are pruned; unloaded blocks are retained.


PERMISSIONS
-----------

- ezvac.use (default true): help, temperature, list, info, and settings-panel use.
- ezvac.admin (default op): create, change, link, remove, sync, and stats.

See commands.txt for the full command reference.
